The Story

Built for a friend. Designed for everyone who bets.

WagerIQ started with a conversation in State College, Pennsylvania, and a problem that had no good solution.

Founder's Note

A friend's headache became the motivation to build something real

My name is Adam Lieb. I'm a student at Penn State University studying Integrative Science, a program that trains you to look at complex systems and find practical solutions at the intersection of data, science, and real-world application.

A close friend of mine placed bets throughout the year. Nothing unusual for a college student in a state where sports betting is fully legal. When tax season rolled around, he came to me completely lost. He had winnings and losses spread across four different apps, no clear record of any of it, and no idea what the IRS actually required of him.

Watching him try to piece it together was frustrating. Every platform reported data differently. Some showed net results, others showed gross payouts. Nothing was standardized, and there was no tool designed to help someone in his situation figure it out without hiring an accountant.

I realized this wasn't just his problem. It was the problem for anyone who bets recreationally and tries to do the right thing at tax time. The tools that exist were built for professionals, not students and everyday bettors.

So I built WagerIQ. The goal is simple: give anyone who bets, whether they win or lose, a fast, accurate, and honest way to understand what they owe and file with confidence.

Adam Lieb
Founder, Penn State University, Integrative Science
Penn State Eberly College of Science
